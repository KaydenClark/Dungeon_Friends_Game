# Dungeon Friends - World Lore

**Status:** unified-world pivot story spine; open details are intentionally
unlocked
**Purpose:** develop Selena, the dragon, the city, the expedition, regions, and
Dungeon Friends without overloading `BLUEPRINT.md`

`BLUEPRINT.md` owns product and architecture. If a story choice changes build
order, mechanics, data shape, or a locked decision, reconcile it into the
Blueprint and `TASKBOARD.md`.

## Core Premise

A dragon has landed atop the mountain overlooking the city. Its presence is
visible from early in the adventure and has changed the surrounding region.
The goddess Selena selects the player to lead the mission to remove it.

The player is not chosen because they are the strongest fighter. Their role is
to find, recruit, and coordinate people whose powers solve different parts of
the journey. The expedition succeeds because the Dungeon Friends learn to
combine their abilities, not because one hero becomes self-sufficient.

Working story promise:

> The dragon is always above you, the mountain is always ahead of you, and
> every friend changes how you can reach it.

## Adventure Spine

The repeated regional loop is:

> see a local consequence -> meet the people affected -> identify a problem ->
> recruit or deepen a friendship -> combine abilities to resolve the problem ->
> leave the world changed -> open the next path toward the mountain

Encounters may be battles, environmental crises, deliveries, escorts,
negotiations, rescues, or mixed objectives. Combat should resolve a threat,
not exist only to distribute XP.

## Opening Thesis Slice

The first story-complete prototype should remain deliberately small:

- The city and dragon are visible or clearly established.
- Selena gives the player the expedition mission.
- The player begins with the Hero and meets two real Dungeon Friends.
- One friend supplies a Growth-oriented field ability.
- One friend supplies Flame, Water, Force, or Air so a combination can be
  demonstrated.
- One NPC has a noncombat problem that remains resolved.
- One environmental obstacle requires the same material vocabulary used in
  the tactical encounter.
- One visible enemy threat can be avoided, confronted, and permanently
  defeated.
- Resolving the slice opens a path pointing toward the mountain.

Names, species, classes, and personalities for the first two friends are open
until their gameplay verbs and relationship are designed together.

**Thesis-slice canon (D-044, flagged per D-038):** the Growth friend is
**Wren**, a quiet forager-botanist of the Foothills/Forest who stayed behind
as the dragon's shadow withered the grove she tends. She will not fight for
sport - "things want to live, mostly" - but joins the expedition to regrow
what the dragon's presence ruins. Her route home is the Withered Grove
(`docs/planning/S004_THESIS_ROUTE.md`): a forager's dead herb bed resolved
without combat, a vine-gate puzzle, one squatting slime guardian, and a grove
heart that stays regrown. The second (elemental) friend, and every open dragon
and Selena question above, remain open; the thesis slice scopes to one
recruit per S-004.

## Dungeon Friends

Every friend should answer five questions:

1. Why do they join this expedition?
2. What problem can they solve in the world?
3. What tactical role do they play?
4. Which other friend's ability changes the meaning of theirs?
5. What do they want besides defeating the dragon?

Friends should not be disposable collectible units. The Pokémon-like appeal
comes from discovery and team choice, while each recruit remains a named
character with an authored relationship and story.

Roster guardrail:

- thesis slice: Hero plus two friends;
- Steam demo: five to six recruitable friends;
- full-game planning ceiling: approximately ten to twelve before expansion is
  reconsidered from real production evidence.

## World Shape

Only the opening route and mountain destination are required now. Do not lock a
five-region content plan before the thesis slice proves the game.

Likely world anchors:

- **The City:** the population living in the dragon's shadow and the expedition
  staging point.
- **Selena's Shrine or Sanctuary:** mission framing, party swapping, recovery,
  or save functionality if the gameplay design validates those roles.
- **The Foothills/Forest:** opening systemic playground and recruitment route.
- **Water route:** natural home for Water, Frost, current, bridge, and
  conductivity interactions if it survives scope review.
- **Mountain ascent:** final route that recombines learned abilities and
  relationships.
- **Dragon summit:** culmination, not a separate game with an unrelated ruleset.

Additional villages, rivers, ruins, caves, and regions earn their place by
supporting a friend, a reusable verb, or the mountain expedition.

## The Dragon

Locked:

- It is physically associated with the mountain above the city.
- Its arrival motivates the expedition from the beginning.
- The final confrontation should test party combinations and world verbs.

Open:

- Is the dragon malicious, territorial, wounded, controlled, or responding to
  an older threat?
- Why has it landed now?
- Why can the city or an existing army not remove it?
- Does "remove" necessarily mean kill?
- How has its presence affected fire, water, weather, creatures, trade, or
  access to the mountain?

Do not implement an answer incidentally through level dressing. These choices
shape the emotional meaning of the entire game.

## Selena

Locked:

- Selena is a goddess or is publicly understood as one.
- She selects the player to lead the expedition.
- Her involvement provides a credible party-management and mission frame.

Open:

- Why this player?
- Can Selena communicate freely, only at shrines, or through an emissary?
- Does she understand the dragon's motive?
- Is her instruction trustworthy, incomplete, or contested?
- Does she grant a mechanical coordination gift, or is the player's leadership
  entirely human?

## Former Lore Superseded

The previous mandatory sequence of four legendary items across Forest, River,
Mountain, City, and Dragon Lair is no longer canon. Individual legendary tools
may return if they support the friend-and-expedition story, but the game does
not owe the old count, region order, or item names anything.

## Story Guardrails

- The dragon and mountain remain perceptible enough to anchor the adventure.
- Friends, their abilities, and their relationships drive progression.
- A local problem should produce a visible persistent consequence when solved.
- Do not create a region only to make the world larger.
- Do not write a friend whose mechanics and story can be swapped with anyone
  else without consequence.
- Do not require killing when another resolution would better serve the
  encounter and story.
- Keep story claims proportional to implemented content during the migration.
